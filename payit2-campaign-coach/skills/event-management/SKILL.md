# Event Management

Guide organizers through creating, promoting, and managing events on PayIt2.com. From ticket pricing to post-event follow-up, every detail impacts attendance, revenue, and attendee satisfaction.

## Event Types Supported

- Conferences and workshops
- Parties and celebrations
- Sports events and tournaments
- Concerts and performances
- Community gatherings
- Reunions
- Religious events
- Club/group outings

## Event Page Creation Process

### Step 1: Event Details Interview

Conduct a structured interview to understand the event fully. Ask these questions one at a time:

1. **What type of event is it?** Get the category (conference, party, concert, etc.) and a brief description.
2. **When is it?** Date, start time, end time, and timezone. Ask about multi-day if applicable.
3. **Where is it?** In-person venue (name, address, parking), virtual (platform link), or hybrid. Get specific details attendees need.
4. **What's the capacity?** Maximum attendees. Separate by tier if applicable (e.g., 500 general, 50 VIP).
5. **What will attendees experience?** Agenda, speakers, activities, food/drink, entertainment — anything that sells the ticket.
6. **What do attendees need to know?** Dress code, what to bring, accessibility info, age restrictions, refund policy.

### Step 2: Ticket Tier Strategy

Design 2-4 ticket tiers with 20-25% price gaps between them. The gap must be large enough to feel meaningful but small enough that upgrades feel attainable.

**Early Bird Pricing:**
- Discount: 25-30% off regular price
- Allocation: First 30% of total capacity
- Window: 7-14 days after announcement
- Purpose: Generate early revenue and social proof

**Standard Tier Structure:**
| Tier | Typical Discount | What's Included |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Early Bird | 25-30% off | Same as regular, reward for commitment |
| Regular | Base price | Standard event access |
| VIP / Premium | 50-100% above regular | Priority seating, exclusive access, perks |

**Group Discounts:**
- 5+ tickets: 10% off per ticket
- 10+ tickets: 15% off per ticket
- 20+ tickets: 20% off per ticket

Group discounts are powerful — they turn one buyer into a table, a team, or a section.

### Step 3: Event Description Writing

Write the event description following this structure:

1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences): Lead with the most exciting thing about the event. What makes it unmissable?
2. **What / When / Where** (2-3 sentences): Core logistics in plain language.
3. **Who it's for** (1-2 sentences): Help the right people self-select. "Perfect for..." or "Whether you're a..."
4. **What's included** (bullet list): Spell out everything the ticket gets them — speakers, food, swag, access.
5. **Schedule / Agenda** (if applicable): Time blocks so attendees can plan their day.
6. **Logistics** (1-2 sentences): Parking, transit, what to bring, accessibility.
7. **CTA** (1 sentence): Direct call to action. "Grab your tickets before early bird ends."

Target under 200 words for the main description. Use bullet points and headers for scannability. Attendees skim — make every word earn its place.

### Step 4: Visual Strategy

Advise the organizer on visual content:

- **Hero image**: The single most compelling photo or graphic. It appears in every share preview, so it must communicate the event's energy at thumbnail size.
- **Event photos**: Past event photos showing crowds, energy, and real moments. Authentic beats polished.
- **Venue photos**: Help attendees visualize where they're going. Especially important for new venues.
- **Branded graphics**: Consistent colors, fonts, and logo placement. Include date and location on every graphic.
- **Speaker/performer headshots**: If the lineup sells the event, show the faces.

### Step 5: Promotion Plan

Launch promotion 4-6 weeks before the event. Events need more lead time than fundraisers because attendees must commit calendar time, not just dollars.

**Key stat**: 61% of millennials discover events through social media. Prioritize visual, shareable content.

**Promotion channels:**
- **Social media**: Countdown posts, behind-the-scenes, speaker spotlights, ticket giveaways
- **Email campaigns**: Segmented blasts to past attendees, subscribers, and partner lists
- **Partner/sponsor cross-promotion**: Co-branded posts, shared email lists, affiliate ticket links
- **Countdown-based urgency**: Unlike fundraisers (goal-based urgency), events use time-based urgency — the date is the deadline

See the full promotion timeline below.

### Step 6: Page Optimization

Before publishing, run through this pre-publish checklist:

- [ ] Date and time are correct (including timezone)
- [ ] Location is complete with address and directions
- [ ] All ticket tiers are configured with correct prices and capacities
- [ ] Description is under 200 words and follows the hook-details-CTA structure
- [ ] Hero image is uploaded and looks good at thumbnail size
- [ ] Payment processing is connected and tested
- [ ] Confirmation email content is customized
- [ ] Social sharing is enabled with correct preview image
- [ ] Refund/cancellation policy is stated
- [ ] Contact info for questions is visible

## Attendee Communication Cadence

Consistent, well-timed communication turns ticket buyers into excited attendees and reduces no-shows.

### Registration Confirmation (Immediate)
- Thank them for registering
- Confirm event details (date, time, location, ticket tier)
- Set expectations — what to look forward to
- Include calendar invite link (.ics or Google Calendar)
- Provide contact info for questions

### 1 Week Before
- Logistics reminder: venue address, parking, transit options
- Updated schedule or agenda
- What to bring (ID, printed ticket, comfortable shoes, etc.)
- Build anticipation — highlight a speaker, activity, or surprise

### 3 Days Before
- Excitement builder: "We can't wait to see you!"
- Final details or last-minute changes
- Share with friends prompt — "Know someone who'd love this? There are still tickets available."
- Social media handles and event hashtag

### Day Before
- Final reminder with all logistics in one place
- Weather-appropriate advice if outdoor
- Parking/check-in specifics
- "See you tomorrow!" energy

### Day Of
- Welcome message: "It's here! Here's everything you need."
- Real-time updates if needed (gate changes, schedule adjustments)
- Social media prompts — hashtag, photo spots, tag us

### 24-48 Hours After
- Thank you for attending
- Feedback survey link (keep it to 3-5 questions)
- Photo gallery or highlight reel link
- Tease the next event if applicable

### 1-2 Weeks After
- Highlights recap with photos and stats (X attendees, X moments)
- Early access or discount for the next event
- Community follow-up — invite to group, newsletter, or social channels

## Promotion Timeline

### 6 Weeks Out
- Announce the event publicly
- Open early bird ticket sales
- Launch social media presence (event page, first posts)
- Send email blast to existing list

### 4 Weeks Out
- Close early bird pricing
- Open regular pricing
- Second email blast with lineup/agenda details
- Begin partner/sponsor cross-promotion

### 2 Weeks Out
- Social proof messaging: "[X] tickets sold — don't miss out"
- Urgency: "Only [X] tickets remaining" or "2 weeks left"
- Targeted email to opened-but-didn't-buy segment
- Speaker/performer spotlight content

### 1 Week Out
- "Last chance" messaging across all channels
- Direct outreach to undecided (personal emails, DMs)
- Final partner push
- Logistics email to registered attendees

### Day Of
- Live social media coverage (stories, posts, live streams)
- Real-time attendee updates
- Encourage attendee-generated content (hashtag, photo contests)

### Post-Event
- Thank you emails within 48 hours
- Photo/video sharing within 1 week
- Feedback survey
- Next event announcement with early access pricing

## Key Statistics

Reference these data points when coaching organizers:

- **61% of millennials** discover events through social media
- **BOGO offers** sell 2.2x more tickets than standard pricing
- Events with **early bird tiers** sell 30% of capacity before regular pricing opens
- **Group discounts** increase average order value by 40-60%
- Attendees who receive **3+ pre-event emails** are 25% less likely to no-show
- Post-event surveys with **3-5 questions** get 3x the response rate of longer surveys
- Events promoted **4-6 weeks out** sell 70% more tickets than those promoted 2 weeks out
