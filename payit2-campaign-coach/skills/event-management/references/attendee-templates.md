# Attendee Email Templates

Every template below includes a subject line, body with bracketed placeholders, and tone notes. Replace all `[bracketed]` text with event-specific details before sending.

---

## 1. Registration Confirmation

**Subject**: You're in! Your [Event Name] registration is confirmed

**Send**: Immediately upon registration

> Hi [First Name],
>
> You're officially registered for **[Event Name]**! We're excited to have you.
>
> **Here are your details:**
> - **Event**: [Event Name]
> - **Date**: [Date] at [Start Time] - [End Time] [Timezone]
> - **Location**: [Venue Name], [Address] *(or: Virtual — link will be sent before the event)*
> - **Ticket**: [Tier Name] — [What's included in 1 sentence]
> - **Confirmation #**: [Confirmation Number]
>
> **Add to your calendar**: [Calendar Invite Link]
>
> **What to expect**: [1-2 sentences about what makes this event special — the highlight, the vibe, or the main takeaway].
>
> Questions? Reply to this email or reach us at [Contact Email].
>
> See you there!
> [Organizer Name]
> [Event Name] Team

**Tone**: Warm, clear, and organized. This is a transactional email but should still feel personal, not robotic.

## 2. One Week Reminder

**Subject**: [Event Name] is one week away — here's what you need to know

**Send**: 7 days before event

> Hi [First Name],
>
> **[Event Name] is just one week away!** Here's everything you need to plan your [day/evening]:
>
> **When**: [Date], [Start Time] - [End Time] [Timezone]
>
> **Where**: [Venue Name], [Full Address]
> - **Parking**: [Parking details — lot, garage, street, cost]
> - **Transit**: [Nearest transit stop or rideshare drop-off point]
> - **Entrance**: [Which door, check-in process]
>
> **Schedule**:
> - [Time] — [Activity/Session]
> - [Time] — [Activity/Session]
> - [Time] — [Activity/Session]
>
> **What to bring**: [List — ID, ticket (printed or mobile), comfortable shoes, business cards, etc.]
>
> **Highlight**: [One exciting thing to look forward to — keynote speaker, special guest, activity, food, etc.]
>
> Can't wait to see you next [day of week]!
> [Organizer Name]

**Tone**: Helpful and practical. This email reduces anxiety by answering logistics questions before they're asked.

## 4. Day-Before Reminder

**Subject**: Tomorrow! Everything you need for [Event Name]

**Send**: Day before event, ideally by noon

> Hi [First Name],
>
> **[Event Name] is TOMORROW!** Here's your all-in-one info sheet:
>
> **Date**: [Date]
> **Time**: [Start Time] - [End Time]
> **Location**: [Venue Name], [Address]
> **Check-in**: [Check-in process — where to go, what to show]
>
> **Getting there**:
> - **Driving**: [Parking details and cost]
> - **Transit/Rideshare**: [Drop-off point or nearest stop]
>
> [If outdoor event: **Weather**: The forecast calls for [weather]. We recommend [dress/gear suggestion].]
>
> **Your ticket**: [How to access — mobile, printed, will-call]
>
> **One last thing**: [Brief exciting teaser — "We have a few surprises planned" or "The lineup is incredible"]
>
> See you tomorrow!
> [Organizer Name]

**Tone**: Clear, practical, and confident. This is the "everything in one place" email. Attendees should be able to reference just this email on event day.

## 5. Day-Of Welcome

**Subject**: It's here! Welcome to [Event Name]

**Send**: Morning of event (2-3 hours before start)

> Hi [First Name],
>
> **Today's the day!** Welcome to [Event Name].
>
> **Quick reference**:
> - **Doors open**: [Time]
> - **Address**: [Venue Name], [Address]
> - **Check-in**: [Where to go when you arrive]
> - **Wi-Fi**: [Network name] / [Password] *(if applicable)*
>
> **While you're here**:
> - Share your experience: **#[EventHashtag]** on [platforms]
> - Tag us: **[Social Media Handle]**
> - [Photo spot, activity, or interactive element to mention]
>
> **Need help?** Look for team members in [identifier — colored shirts, lanyards, at the info desk] or text [Phone Number].
>
> Have an amazing [day/evening]!
> [Organizer Name]

**Tone**: High energy but brief. People are getting ready — give them only what they need to walk in confidently.

## 6. Post-Event Thank You

**Subject**: Thank you for being part of [Event Name]!

**Send**: 24-48 hours after event

> Hi [First Name],
>
> **Thank you for coming to [Event Name]!** Having you there made it special.
>
> We had [X] incredible [attendees/guests/participants] and [highlight stat or moment — "raised $X for [cause]" or "heard from 12 amazing speakers" or "competed in 8 rounds"].
>
> **We'd love your feedback** — it takes less than 2 minutes and helps us make the next one even better:
> [Survey Link]
>
> **Photos and highlights** will be shared at [link or "on our social channels"] within the next few days. Stay tuned!
>
> [If applicable: **Missed a session?** Recordings will be available at [Link] by [Date].]
>
> Thank you for being part of this. We hope to see you again soon.
>
> Gratefully,
> [Organizer Name]
> [Event Name] Team

**Tone**: Grateful and warm. This closes the loop. The survey ask should feel like a favor, not a demand.

## 9. Cancellation / Change Notification

**Subject**: Important update about [Event Name]

**Send**: As soon as change is confirmed

> Hi [First Name],
>
> We're writing with an important update about **[Event Name]**.
>
> **[Choose one]:**
>
> *For date/time change:*
> The event has been moved to **[New Date]** at **[New Time]**. The location remains the same: [Venue Name], [Address]. We apologize for any inconvenience.
>
> *For venue change:*
> The event location has changed to **[New Venue Name]**, [New Address]. The date and time remain the same: [Date] at [Time].
>
> *For cancellation:*
> We've made the difficult decision to cancel [Event Name]. [1-2 sentences explaining why — weather, unforeseen circumstances, etc.].
>
> **What this means for you**:
> - [If change: "Your registration is still valid. No action needed on your end."]
> - [If cancellation: "A full refund of $[Amount] will be processed to your original payment method within [X] business days."]
> - [If rescheduled: "We're working on a new date and will share it as soon as it's confirmed. Your ticket will transfer automatically."]
>
> We understand this is frustrating and we appreciate your patience. If you have questions, please reach out to [Contact Email] or [Phone Number].
>
> Sincerely,
> [Organizer Name]
> [Event Name] Team

**Tone**: Direct, empathetic, and professional. Lead with the facts, then address their concerns. Never bury the lede.

## 10. Waitlist Notification

**Subject**: A spot just opened at [Event Name] — claim yours now

**Send**: When a ticket becomes available from cancellation or capacity increase

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Great news — **a [Tier Name] ticket just became available** for [Event Name]!
>
> **Event**: [Event Name]
> **Date**: [Date] at [Time]
> **Location**: [Venue Name], [Address]
> **Ticket**: [Tier Name] — $[Price]
>
> **You have 24 hours to claim this spot** before it's offered to the next person on the waitlist.
>
> **[Claim Your Ticket: Link]**
>
> If you're no longer interested, no action is needed — the spot will automatically move to the next person after 24 hours.
>
> Hope to see you there!
> [Organizer Name]

**Tone**: Urgent but friendly. The 24-hour window creates action without pressure. Make it easy to say yes.
