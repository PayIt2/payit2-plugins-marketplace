# Group Collection

Guide organizers through collecting payments from a group of people on PayIt2.com. Whether it's team dues, trip costs, group gifts, or event expenses, the key to successful group collection is clarity, easy payment, and respectful follow-up.

## Collection Page Setup

### Step 1: Collection Details Interview

Conduct a structured interview to understand the organizer's collection needs. Ask these questions one at a time, building on each answer:

1. **What's the collection for?** Get the specific purpose — team jerseys, cabin rental, birthday gift, club dues, etc.
2. **How many people are in the group?** Get the exact or estimated headcount.
3. **What's the total amount needed?** Get the real number, including any buffer for fees or overages.
4. **Is there a deadline?** Understand the timeline — when does the money actually need to be collected by, and why.
5. **Is the amount per person fixed or flexible?** Fixed = everyone pays the same. Flexible = suggested amount with room to adjust.
6. **Are there tiers or options?** Different room types, meal choices, participation levels, etc.

### Step 2: Page Configuration

Build the collection page with these elements:

- **Title**: Clear and specific. Use the format: "[Group Name] [Purpose] Collection"
  - Good: "Soccer Team Spring Jersey Order"
  - Good: "Johnson Family Reunion Cabin Fund"
  - Bad: "Please Pay" or "Money Collection"
- **Description**: Include what the collection is for, how much each person owes, the deadline, and what happens with the funds. Keep it under 100 words.
- **Goal amount**: Set to the total amount needed. This lets contributors see progress toward the target.
- **Deadline**: Set a firm date tied to a real constraint (booking deadline, order cutoff, event date).

### Step 3: Cost Structure

Choose the right model for the collection:

#### Fixed Per-Person Split

Everyone pays the same amount. Best for: dues, equal splits, uniform orders.

| Total Cost | Group Size | Per Person |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| $500 | 10 people | $50.00 |
| $1,200 | 15 people | $80.00 |
| $3,000 | 25 people | $120.00 |
| $5,000 | 40 people | $125.00 |

**Calculation**: Total cost ÷ number of people = per-person amount. Round up to the nearest dollar to create a small buffer for fees.

#### Tiered Options

Different levels for different participation. Best for: trips with room choices, events with meal options, equipment with size variations.

Example — Weekend Trip:
- Shared room: $150/person
- Private room: $250/person
- Day-only (no room): $75/person

Set up each tier as a separate option on the collection page with clear labels.

#### Flexible / Voluntary

Suggested amount with the option to give more or less. Best for: group gifts, community projects, optional contributions.

Set the suggested amount prominently in the description. Example: "We're suggesting $25 per person, but any amount is appreciated."

### Step 4: Communication Plan

Plan the outreach before launching the collection:

1. **Who to notify**: List every person who needs to pay. Get accurate contact information.
2. **Primary channel**: Where does this group already communicate? (Group text, email, Slack, Facebook group, etc.)
3. **Draft the launch message**: Write it before publishing the page. See `references/collection-templates.md` for templates.
4. **Set the reminder cadence**: Follow the Friendly Collector Framework below.
5. **Assign a co-organizer**: Having a second person helps with follow-up and adds credibility.

### Step 5: Tracking & Transparency

Keep the group informed without being pushy:

- **Share progress milestones**: "We're at 50%!" or "12 of 20 people have paid!"
- **Thank payers publicly** (with permission): "Thanks to everyone who's already paid!"
- **Never single out non-payers publicly**: Follow up via private message only.
- **Celebrate when you hit the goal**: Send a group message acknowledging everyone's contribution.
- **Post a summary**: After the collection closes, share what was collected and how it will be used.

## The "Friendly Collector" Framework

Key principle: Frame every reminder as a progress update, not an accusation. The organizer should never feel like a debt collector, and contributors should never feel shamed.

### Reminder Cadence

**Day 0 — Launch Message**:
> "Hey everyone! Here's the link to pay for [X]. Details inside. Deadline is [date]."

Send to all channels. Keep it brief, friendly, informational.

**Day 3 — Progress Update**:
> "We're at 40%! Thanks to everyone who's already paid. Quick reminder for the rest — here's the link."

Lead with gratitude and progress. The link does the work.

**Day 7 (or midpoint) — Momentum Update**:
> "Over halfway there! [X] of [Y] people have paid. If you haven't yet, here's the link."

Social proof drives action. Show that most people have already paid.

**3 Days Before Deadline — Urgency**:
> "Deadline is [date]. We still need [X] people to pay so we can [book the venue / order the gear / etc.]."

Connect the deadline to a real consequence. Make it concrete.

**Deadline Day — Final Nudge**:
> "Last day! We need [amount] more to hit our goal. Please pay today if you haven't."

Short, direct, no guilt. Just the facts and the link.

**Post-Deadline — Straggler Follow-Up**:
> "We're finalizing payments. If you still need to pay, please do so by [grace date]."

Send as a private message, not to the group. Give a brief grace period (2-3 days).

### Cadence Rules

- **Maximum 5-6 messages total** to the full group. More than that breeds annoyance.
- **Start collecting 2-3 weeks** before the money is actually needed. This gives enough runway for the full cadence.
- **Private follow-ups only** after the deadline. Never call out non-payers in the group.

## Group Collection Types

### Team / Club Dues
- **Frequency**: Recurring (monthly, quarterly, seasonal)
- **Amount type**: Fixed per person
- **Key tip**: Set up the page once and reuse the link each period. Include what dues cover in the description.

### Trip / Travel Collection
- **Frequency**: One-time
- **Amount type**: Often tiered (room types, activity packages)
- **Key tip**: Collect at least 3 weeks before any booking deadlines. Break down costs so people see exactly what they're paying for.

### Group Gifts
- **Frequency**: One-time
- **Amount type**: Flexible / voluntary
- **Key tip**: Keep the gift a surprise if needed — use a vague title visible to the recipient. Set a suggested amount but emphasize "any amount is appreciated."

### Event Costs
- **Frequency**: One-time
- **Amount type**: Fixed or tiered
- **Key tip**: Itemize costs (venue, catering, decorations, entertainment) so contributors see where money goes.

### Sports Equipment / Uniforms
- **Frequency**: Seasonal
- **Amount type**: Fixed per item
- **Key tip**: Collect sizes and preferences alongside payment. Include a clear order deadline.

### Classroom / School Fees
- **Frequency**: Varies
- **Amount type**: Fixed or flexible
- **Key tip**: Be specific about what the funds cover. Parents respond well to transparency and a clear list of items/activities.

### HOA / Neighborhood Collections
- **Frequency**: One-time or periodic
- **Amount type**: Fixed assessment
- **Key tip**: Reference any governing documents or vote results that authorized the collection. Formality matters here.

### Religious Contributions
- **Frequency**: Varies
- **Amount type**: Flexible / tithe-based
- **Key tip**: Frame as an invitation, not an obligation. Emphasize the shared purpose and community impact.

## Best Practices

These principles apply to every group collection, regardless of type:

1. **Pay your own share first.** It sets the norm and shows commitment. People follow the organizer's lead.
2. **Set a firm deadline tied to a real constraint.** "We need to book by Friday" is stronger than "please pay soon."
3. **Be transparent about where the money goes.** Provide an itemized breakdown in the description.
4. **Make it easy.** One link, multiple payment methods. Remove every possible friction point.
5. **Celebrate progress publicly.** "We hit 75%!" creates momentum and encourages the remaining group to act.
6. **Never single out non-payers publicly.** Private messages only. Respect and relationships matter more than speed.
7. **Send max 5-6 messages total.** The reminder cadence above is the maximum. More messages = diminishing returns + annoyance.
8. **Start collecting 2-3 weeks early.** Give yourself runway. Last-minute collections have the lowest completion rates.
